Sensorized Insole for Ground Reaction Force & Center of Pressure Estimation

In this project, I designed and built a wearable sensorized insole capable of estimating 3D Ground Reaction Forces (GRFs) and Center of Pressure (CoP) during gait. The system integrates a custom pressure-sensing insole and an on-board inertial measurement unit, embedded into a compact 3D-printed enclosure attached to the shoe.

To enable field-ready gait analysis, the prototype continuously acquires pressure and acceleration data, streams it through a custom firmware pipeline, and reconstructs GRF and CoP using a Bidirectional LSTM network trained on synchronized force-plate data.

Beyond overall performance, we analyzed:

  • how estimation accuracy changes with pressure-only vs. pressure + acceleration inputs,
  • whether a low-density, low-cost insole can approximate laboratory-grade force plate measurements,
  • the feasibility of using this system for outside-the-lab gait assessment.

This work was published as a paper and oral presentation at IEEE STAR 2024,
“A Sensorized Insole to Estimate Ground Reaction Forces and Center of Pressure During Gait”.
