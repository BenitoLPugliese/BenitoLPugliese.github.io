Sensorized Insole for Ground Reaction Force & Center of Pressure Estimation

I designed and built a wearable sensorized insole capable of estimating 3D Ground Reaction Forces (GRFs) and Center of Pressure (CoP) during gait. The system integrates a custom pressure-sensing insole and an on-board inertial measurement unit, packaged in a compact 3D-printed enclosure attached to the shoe.

To enable field-ready gait analysis, the device continuously acquires pressure and acceleration data, streams them through a custom firmware pipeline, and reconstructs GRF and CoP using a bidirectional LSTM model trained on synchronized force-plate measurements.

My Contributions:
  • Designed the wearable hardware architecture (pressure insole + IMU + enclosure)
  • Developed firmware for continuous data acquisition and storage
  • Built the end-to-end data pipeline from sensing to inference
  • Trained and evaluated a bidirectional LSTM for GRF and CoP estimation
  • Conducted validation against laboratory-grade force plates
Beyond overall accuracy, this work investigated:
  • how estimation performance changes with pressure-only vs. pressure + inertial inputs,
  • whether a low-density, low-cost insole can approximate force-plate measurements,
  • the feasibility of out-of-the-lab gait assessment using wearable sensing.

Outcome: Demonstrated that wearable pressure–IMU fusion combined with machine learning can reliably estimate GRF and CoP in real-world walking scenarios.

This work was published as a paper and oral presentation at IEEE STAR 2024,
“A Sensorized Insole to Estimate Ground Reaction Forces and Center of Pressure During Gait”.
