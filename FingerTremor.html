Wearable Finger Tremor Monitor for Parkinson’s Disease

Purpose: Tremor is one of the most recognizable motor symptoms of Parkinson’s disease (PD) and is typically assessed through brief clinical exams and ordinal rating scales, which provide only coarse snapshots of a highly dynamic symptom. This project explored a wearable system to obtain high-resolution, quantitative finger tremor metrics during intervention studies.

System Overview: A lightweight multi-sensor wearable was developed by extending a Shimmer3 research platform to support additional fingertip-mounted accelerometers, enabling independent tremor capture from multiple fingers during controlled clinical protocols.

My Role:
  • Extended an existing wearable research platform to a multi-sensor finger-level configuration
  • Modified firmware to acquire and synchronize data from additional accelerometer channels
  • Designed the data acquisition and offline analysis pipeline for tremor quantification
  • Supported human-subject studies evaluating vibration-based tremor intervention

Outcome: The system enabled high-resolution tremor metrics beyond standard clinical scales and was successfully deployed in a clinical study setting.

This work was presented as a poster at International Congress of Parkinson’s Disease and Movement Disorders (MDS) 2023, Copenhagen (Denmark), August 27-31, titled "Use of a Wearable Vibration-Based Device for the Treatment of Parkinson’s Disease Tremor".
Authors: Stephen C, Fabara E, Parisi F, Pugliese BL, Vergara-Diaz G, Gochyyev P, Davanzo A, Carballo D, Pina K, Luu T, Bonato P.

Prototype in Use

Prototype setup: three fingertip accelerometers connected to a modified Shimmer3 unit via the Proto3 expansion board, used to quantify finger tremor in a participant with Parkinson’s disease.
