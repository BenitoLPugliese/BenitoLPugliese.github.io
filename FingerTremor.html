Wearable Finger Tremor Monitor for Parkinson’s Disease

Tremor is one of the most recognizable motor symptoms of Parkinson’s disease (PD) and can significantly affect fine-motor function and quality of life. Clinically, tremor severity is usually assessed through brief in-clinic exams and ordinal rating scales, which provide only a coarse snapshot of a highly dynamic symptom.

This project explores a wearable system to quantify finger tremor using lightweight accelerometers mounted on the fingertips and a modified Shimmer3 sensing platform. The goal was to obtain high-resolution, quantitative tremor metrics during a protocol designed to test the effectiveness of a mechanical vibration–based intervention for tremor reduction.

I was responsible for the hardware modification, firmware development, data acquisition pipeline, and data analysis, adapting an existing research device to a new multi-sensor configuration suitable for clinical studies.

System Overview

The system is built around a Shimmer3 unit, which was extended using the Proto3 expansion board to access unused microcontroller pins and interface with additional sensors. Three small wired accelerometers were mounted on the index, middle, and ring fingertips to capture finger tremor independently.

On the firmware side, I modified the Shimmer3 firmware to:

  • Read synchronized data from three additional accelerometer channels,
  • Store the combined data locally for offline analysis.

This work was presented as a poster at International Congress of Parkinson’s Disease and Movement Disorders (MDS) 2023, Copenhagen (Denmark), August 27-31, titled "Use of a Wearable Vibration-Based Device for the Treatment of Parkinson’s Disease Tremor".
Authors: Stephen C, Fabara E, Parisi F, Pugliese BL, Vergara-Diaz G, Gochyyev P, Davanzo A, Carballo D, Pina K, Luu T, Bonato P.

Prototype in Use

Prototype setup: three fingertip accelerometers connected to a modified Shimmer3 unit via the Proto3 expansion board, used to quantify finger tremor in a participant with Parkinson’s disease.
