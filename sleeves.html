Sensorized Sleeves for Adaptive Knee OA Rehabilitation

Knee osteoarthritis (OA) is among the leading causes of disability worldwide, characterized by pain, stiffness, and inflammation that progressively limit mobility and quality of life. While there is no cure, physical activity remains a cornerstone of conservative management, helping preserve function and slow disease progression.

However, the relationship between exercise and symptoms is delicate: insufficient activity limits benefits, while over-exercise can exacerbate pain, swelling, and inflammation. In clinical practice, identifying the “optimal” exercise dose often relies on trial and error, guided primarily by patient-reported symptoms and clinician judgment.

My work focuses on sensorized knee sleeves that objectively monitor knee biomechanics and physiology during exercise. By capturing changes in flexion–extension kinematics, thermal patterns related to inflammation, and bioimpedance signatures of tissue composition, these systems aim to enable data-driven, adaptive rehabilitation programs that adjust exercise dosage proactively before symptoms flare.

The following projects explore three complementary sensing approaches integrated into wearable knee sleeves.

Polymer Optical Fiber Sleeve for Knee Flexion–Extension Estimation

Purpose: Estimate knee flexion–extension during gait using soft, textile-compatible polimer optical fiber (POF) sensors.

System: Sleeve integrating five POF sensors aligned with anatomical structures. LSTM-based estimation model trained against motion-capture ground truth.

My Contributions:
  • Contributed to hardware integration, including power regulation and sensor conditioning
  • Designed data collection pipeline
  • Conducted human-subject validation (MoCap reference)
  • Developed LSTM estimation model
  • Ran analyses on sensor count, placement combinations, and per-cycle error

Outcome: High-accuracy angle estimation from soft, textile-compatible POF sensors, demonstrating promise for rehabilitation monitoring.

This work was published in IEEE Transactions on Neural Systems and Rehabilitation Engineering (TNSRE) and received the 2024 Best Paper Award.

Thermal Sensor Sleeve for Monitoring Knee Inflammation via Temperature Distribution

Purpose: Quantify lateral knee temperature distribution during exercise and recovery as a proxy of inflammation for knee osteoarthritis rehabilitation.

System: A 12-sensor thermal array integrated into a textile sleeve, featuring:
  • Precision thermistors arranged in an anatomically informed layout
  • Multithreaded BLE firmware with real-time streaming
  • GUI for acquisition and visualization
  • Calibration pipelines and thermal compensation strategies
  • Embedded data-logging modes
My Contributions:
  • Architected the full system (hardware integration, firmware, GUI, calibration)
  • Designed sensor layout and mounting within the textile
  • Developed BLE acquisition and synchronization logic
  • Conducted human-subject experiments and validated against a FLIR thermal camera
  • Ran simulations on sensor placement, misalignment tolerance, and sensor density

Outcome: Demonstrated feasibility of monitoring inflammation-related thermal patterns using a using a wearable sensing system, with accuracy comparable to thermal cameras.

This work was published in IEEE Access.

Bioimpedance and Dual IMUs Sleeve for Multimodal Knee Monitoring

Purpose: Monitor tissue impedance and knee flexion–extension during rehabilitation to quantify tissue recovery and joint loading.

System: A textile-integrated knee sleeve featuring:
  • Single- and multifrequency BioZ front end for tissue impedance monitoring
  • Dual IMUs (shank + thigh) for joint kinematics
  • FSM-based BLE firmware with buffering and flash storage
  • Real-time on board kinematic estimation
  • Textile integration of electonics
  • GUI for synchronized acquisition and visualization
My Contributions:
  • Architected the end-to-end system (hardware integration, firmware, GUI, calibration)
  • Designed the PCB and integrated BioZ analog front end
  • Developed multithreaded BLE firmware with finite-state machine
  • Implemented onboard flash logging and real-time joint-angle estimation
  • Integrated electronics into textile sleeve
  • Conducted human-subject validation against motion capture

Outcome: Accurate joint-angle estimation and impedance tracking in real settings, enabling longitudinal monitoring of knee tissue response to exercise.

This work was presented as a poster at IEEE Body Sensor Networks (BSN) 2025, Los Angeles (Nov 3–5), titled "Wearable Bioimpedance Sleeve with Integrated IMUs for Adaptive Knee Osteoarthritis Rehabilitation".
Authors: Benito Lorenzo Pugliese, Federico De Carlo, Andrea Orlando, Mattia Ghibaudo, Marco Caruso, Michelle Bruneau, Danilo Demarchi, Alessandra Angelucci, Andrea Aliverti, Adam Tenforde, Paolo Bonato.
