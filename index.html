Benito L. Pugliese, PhD
Embedded Systems Engineer creating wearable systems to make the invisible visible.

About

I build Wearable Technologies that bridge human physiology, biomechanics, and engineering. My work spans thermal and bioimpedance sensing, polymer-optical-fiber systems, motion tracking, and beyond, always driven by curiosity for new modalities. I design hardware, develop BLE firmware, create real-time software pipelines, and analyze complex physiological and biomechanical data to turn raw signals into actionable insights.

I trained in Electronic Engineering, embedded systems at Politecnico di Torino, and for the past five years I have worked at Spaulding Rehabilitation Hospital's Motion Analysis Lab, Harvard Medical School, in Boston. This environment has exposed me to a wide spectrum of innovative technologies—wearables, robotics, markerless biomechanics, motion capture, and remote monitoring tools—and has given me extensive hands-on experience running studies both in the lab and the community, including countless data-collection sessions with patients and athletes.

My PhD bridged sensor development at Politecnico di Torino with real-world clinical needs at Spaulding Rehabilitation Hospital, focusing on creating wearable systems that make rehabilitation more personalized, adaptive, and measurable.

Currently, as a Research Fellow at Spaulding Rehabilitation Hospital’s Motion Analysis Lab, I lead the development of next-generation systems for digital health and rehabilitation, guiding projects from concept to prototype, validation, and real-world deployment.
