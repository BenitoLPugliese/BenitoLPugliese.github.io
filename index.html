About

I build wearable microsystems that bridge embedded electronics, human physiology, biomechanics, and rehabilitation. My work focuses on textile-integrated sleeves and insoles that combine thermal sensing arrays, bioimpedance front ends, IMUs, polymer optical fibers, and pressure sensors to monitor joint function and tissue health in real-world conditions.

I design PCBs and embedded architectures, develop multithreaded BLE firmware, create lightweight GUIs for device control and real-time visualization, and build analysis pipelines to turn raw signals into actionable biomechanical and physiological metrics.

I trained in Electronics / Embedded Systems Engineering at Politecnico di Torino, and for the past six years I have worked at Spaulding Rehabilitation Hospital’s Motion Analysis Lab (Harvard Medical School) in Boston. This environment has exposed me to a wide range of innovative technologies, wearables, robotics, motion capture, remote monitoring, and given me extensive hands-on experience running human studies with patients and athletes.

Currently, as a Research Fellow at Spaulding’s Motion Analysis Lab, I lead the development of next-generation wearable systems for knee osteoarthritis and neurological conditions, guiding projects from concept to prototype, validation against gold-standard instruments, and deployment in real rehabilitation settings.
